About Twitter Cabinet

In January, Presidential Candidate Donald Trump and Fox News had a falling out around Trump’s decision not to appear at a Presidential Debate. The candidate had polled his Twitter followers prior to the debate to determine whether he should attend. Feeling snubbed, a Fox spokeswoman said the following: “a nefarious source tells us that Trump has his own secret plan to replace the Cabinet with his Twitter followers to see if he should even go to those meetings.”

Twitter Cabinet gathers data on the Twitter followers of the presidential candidates Bernie Sanders, Donald Trump, Hillary Clinton, and Ted Cruz in order to determine how they feel about key political issues relevant to the presidential race. By doing this, we hope to attain a representative sample of each network’s ideas and political leanings.

First, the app scrapes the most recent tweets from each of the candidates’ followers. These tweets are then tagged with topics that are related to election. Once the tweets have been sorted by topics, the app runs the tweets through a sentiment analysis engine. With this data, we provide to a nice visualization allowing you to explore the different topic areas and easily compare the sentiment of each candidate’s followers.

This work was done as part of a class assignment for Georgia Tech's CS4464/6465 Fall 2016 (https://github.com/jacobeisenstein/gt-compj-class/). The opinions expressed here, if any, are solely those of the students working on this assignment and not of the Instructor or Georgia Tech.
